The **First Blood** decks are the fastest way to put V:TES in front of new players. This guide covers how to set up a demo at an event (convention, store, GP, meetup) and how to run a ~30-minute guided round that hooks people in without trying to teach the whole game.

> [!NOTE]
> First Blood decks **are not tournament legal**: they're for learning the mechanics. To play a real game they need to be integrated with at least one V5 deck of the same clan. V5 decks, by contrast, are tournament legal (60-card library minimum), although they're not especially competitive out of the box.

## 1. Goal of the demo

The goal isn't to teach V:TES end to end — to really appreciate it you need roughly **ten games** — but to show how a turn flows, how the core interactions feel, and to let players taste the political game, so they leave wanting to come back.

Keep the demo to **30–45 minutes**. A full game runs much longer and loses the showcase effect.

## 2. What you need

- **5 First Blood decks** (Nosferatu, Toreador, Tremere, Malkavian, Ventrue) already unboxed and split by clan.
- **Pool and blood counters** (tokens, coins or dice): 30 per player.

For posters and handouts to hang up or give to attendees, see the [appendix at the bottom](#useful-materials-and-links).

## 3. Table setup

Start with **20 pool**, but with a couple of shortcuts compared to the standard setup, to avoid losing the audience in the first influence-only turns:

- play **open-handed** during the first round, so the demo runner can comment on every card played; from round two you can switch to closed hands if the table can handle it;
- every player starts with **one vampire already in play** with full blood (= the vampire's capacity);
- every player must have a **master in hand** that's playable on turn one.

## 5. Guided play sequence

The first round should run roughly like this — don't read it as a script, treat it as a track: the goal is to showcase one different kind of action per clan.

<details>
<summary><strong>Nosferatu</strong> — <em>master economy, bleed, combat</em></summary>

1. Plays <span class="card-hover">Blood Doll<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/blooddoll.jpg" alt="Blood Doll" loading="lazy" /></span>.
2. Bleeds with base <span class="card-hover">Deep Song<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/deepsong.jpg" alt="Deep Song" loading="lazy" /></span>; gets blocked.
3. Plays <span class="card-hover">Roundhouse<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/roundhouse.jpg" alt="Roundhouse" loading="lazy" /></span> as strike; Toreador answers with <span class="card-hover">Majesty<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/majesty.jpg" alt="Majesty" loading="lazy" /></span>.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Toreador</strong> — <em>voting, deal-making, referendum</em></summary>

1. Plays <span class="card-hover">Art Museum<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/artmuseum.jpg" alt="Art Museum" loading="lazy" /></span>.
2. Calls a referendum with <span class="card-hover">Kine Resources Contested<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/kineresourcescontested.jpg" alt="Kine Resources Contested" loading="lazy" /></span>; the Tremere only has <span class="card-hover">Eyes of Argus<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/eyesofargus.jpg" alt="Eyes of Argus" loading="lazy" /></span> in hand as intercept.
3. Pause to explain the **political phase**: who votes (ready vampires, with extra votes from titles), how the referendum's _terms_ work; then let the players discuss and negotiate.
4. Then plays <span class="card-hover">Scalpel Tongue<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/scalpeltongue.jpg" alt="Scalpel Tongue" loading="lazy" /></span>, <span class="card-hover">Voter Captivation<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/votercaptivation.jpg" alt="Voter Captivation" loading="lazy" /></span>, <span class="card-hover">Scorn of Adonis<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/scornofadonis.jpg" alt="Scorn of Adonis" loading="lazy" /></span>.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Tremere</strong> — <em>equip, weapons</em></summary>

1. Plays <span class="card-hover">Chantry<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/chantry.jpg" alt="Chantry" loading="lazy" /></span>.
2. Equips the <span class="card-hover">Sniper Rifle<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/sniperrifle.jpg" alt="Sniper Rifle" loading="lazy" /></span> with an _equip_ action.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Malkavian</strong> — <em>stealth/intercept, bleed redirect</em></summary>

1. Plays <span class="card-hover">The Barrens<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/barrensthe.jpg" alt="The Barrens" loading="lazy" /></span>.
2. Bleeds for 2 with 0 stealth; the Ventrue tries to block.
3. Plays <span class="card-hover">Cloak the Gathering<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/cloakthegathering.jpg" alt="Cloak the Gathering" loading="lazy" /></span> (+1 stealth); the Ventrue answers with <span class="card-hover">Second Tradition: Domain<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/secondtraditiondomain.jpg" alt="Second Tradition: Domain" loading="lazy" /></span> (intercept).
4. Closes with <span class="card-hover">Elder Impersonation<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/elderimpersonation.jpg" alt="Elder Impersonation" loading="lazy" /></span>, which prevents the Ventrue from blocking this action → the 2-bleed goes through.
5. Second action: bleed with <span class="card-hover">Threats<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/threats.jpg" alt="Threats" loading="lazy" /></span>; the Ventrue plays <span class="card-hover">Deflection<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/deflection.jpg" alt="Deflection" loading="lazy" /></span> and redirects the bleed to the next player.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Ventrue</strong> — <em>allies, untap, bleed with bonuses</em></summary>

1. Plays <span class="card-hover">Political Ally<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/politicalally.jpg" alt="Political Ally" loading="lazy" /></span>.
2. Untaps with <span class="card-hover">Freak Drive<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/freakdrive.jpg" alt="Freak Drive" loading="lazy" /></span>.
3. Bleeds with <span class="card-hover">Enchant Kindred<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/enchantkindred.jpg" alt="Enchant Kindred" loading="lazy" /></span>.

</details>

From here the game can carry on freely — the Nosferatu might equip the <span class="card-hover">Raven Spy<img src="https://static.krcg.org/card/ravenspy.jpg" alt="Raven Spy" loading="lazy" /></span>, the Toreador might start voting seriously, and so on. When you sense the group has seen enough, **wrap up the demo**.

> [!TIP]
> When a player makes a "wrong" move (e.g. an impossible block, voting on a referendum they can't participate in), correct it by explaining the **why** of the rule, not just the rule. New players remember the intuition better than the rulebook wording.

## 6. What to avoid

- explaining every rule: stick to 4–5 key concepts per round.
- framing First Blood as a lesser version: it's a taster of the game, not a downgrade.
- getting bogged down on edge cases: defer the details to after the demo and keep the game flowing.

## 7. After the demo

- Talk about the nearest community: who plays, where, when.
- Leave a reference to **vtesitaly.com**, optionally with some informational material.
- If someone shows real interest, take a contact and invite them to the next [local event](/en/comunita/).
- If the event is a **sanctioned Archon Demo**, remember to create it on Archon afterwards (you need to be a Prince or to have a Prince's support): see [Archon Online for Princes](/en/guides/archon-principi/).
- Return the First Blood decks in order — the next person running a demo will thank you.
